# Style Directions

Use one of these directions when the product lacks strong visual language. Adapt tokens and layout to product constraints.

## 1) Editorial Warmth

- Use for: storytelling products, journaling, lifestyle tracking, content-heavy apps
- Palette:
  - Background `#F6F2EA`
  - Surface `#FFFDF8`
  - Text `#1E1A16`
  - Accent `#C35A2E`
  - Accent 2 `#3E6B6F`
- Typography:
  - Headings: `"Fraunces", serif`
  - Body/UI: `"Manrope", sans-serif`
- UI notes:
  - Use generous whitespace and larger heading rhythm.
  - Use subtle paper-like textures or soft gradients.

## 2) Sport Utility

- Use for: fitness, activity dashboards, coaching, habit apps
- Palette:
  - Background `#0D1B2A`
  - Surface `#13263A`
  - Text `#EAF2FB`
  - Accent `#16D978`
  - Accent 2 `#4EC3FF`
- Typography:
  - Headings: `"Space Grotesk", sans-serif`
  - Body/UI: `"IBM Plex Sans", sans-serif`
- UI notes:
  - Use strong data hierarchy and high-contrast status chips.
  - Use directional motion (slide/step) to imply progress.

## 3) Neo Brutalist Product

- Use for: founder products, experimental tools, youth-forward brands
- Palette:
  - Background `#FFF3C9`
  - Surface `#FFFFFF`
  - Text `#111111`
  - Accent `#FF5E5B`
  - Accent 2 `#2D62FF`
- Typography:
  - Headings: `"Bricolage Grotesque", sans-serif`
  - Body/UI: `"Public Sans", sans-serif`
- UI notes:
  - Use high-contrast borders, hard shadows, and bold geometry.
  - Keep interaction states obvious and playful but deterministic.

## 4) Premium Calm

- Use for: productivity, finance, professional SaaS with polished tone
- Palette:
  - Background `#EEF1F4`
  - Surface `#FFFFFF`
  - Text `#1A2433`
  - Accent `#3A6EA5`
  - Accent 2 `#C89B3C`
- Typography:
  - Headings: `"Sora", sans-serif`
  - Body/UI: `"Source Sans 3", sans-serif`
- UI notes:
  - Favor restrained motion and elevated card systems.
  - Balance density with clear sectioning and calm contrast.
